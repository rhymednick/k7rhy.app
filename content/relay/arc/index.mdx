## Voice and character

Relay Arc is the **open sky** in the lineup: **width, shimmer, and clarity** without becoming thin or brittle. It is not warm and clubby like Velvet, and not rude and mid-forward like Torch. The middle **Dream 180** and the **5-way** layout treat the center as a **true core voice** — chords stay **separated and dimensional**, lines feel **lit from within** rather than ice-picky.

Arc should feel **open, ringing, and alive** — spatial clarity for arpeggios, ambient layers, or country-influenced lines that need sparkle and body together. The goal is air and separation that doesn't sacrifice fundamental weight.

## Who builds this

Players who chase **openness, air, and separation** first — clean pop, indie, ambient, and country — anyone who wants **space** in the tone without giving up fundamental weight. If you like delays and washes that fill a room and need a guitar that stays articulate underneath, Arc is aimed at you.

## Configuration

|                   |                                                                 |
| ----------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Bridge**        | Humbucker (Retrotron Liverpool)                                 |
| **Middle**        | Dream 180 — core voice                                          |
| **Neck**          | Humbucker (Vintage 59)                                          |
| **Selector**      | Standard 5-way blade, 2-pole                                    |
| **Master volume** | 500k audio taper, full-size, standard (no push switch)        |
| **Master tone**   | 500k audio taper, full-size, **push-pull**                      |

## Controls

**5-way blade**

1. Bridge  
2. Bridge + middle  
3. Middle  
4. Neck + middle  
5. Neck  

The air contour lives on the tone push function, not in the selector itself.

**Tone (push-pull)** — Engages the Arc air contour.
