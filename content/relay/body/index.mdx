The body is the foundation — print the parts, bond them, let the epoxy cure, then sand and seal the surface. Same body for every voicing.

## Steps

<CardGrid>
    <CardGridItem eyebrow="Step 1" title="Print" href="/relay/body/print">
        Download the print files, choose your material, and get all parts off the bed before moving on.
    </CardGridItem>
    <CardGridItem eyebrow="Step 2" title="Bonding" href="/relay/body/bonding">
        Bond the printed parts into a single complete structure with G/Flex 650 epoxy.
    </CardGridItem>
    <CardGridItem eyebrow="Step 3" title="Finishing" href="/relay/body/finishing">
        Sand the bonded body and seal the surface before hardware installation.
    </CardGridItem>
</CardGrid>

## Materials

<BomSection>
    <BomItem title="PET-CF Filament (×2 spools)" href="https://amzn.to/4uIaVRl" source="Amazon" itemKey="pet-cf-filament" fallback="$66.00" specific substitution="PET-GF (glass fiber filled) is a valid alternative to PET-CF and may be easier to source. Both provide the dimensional stability and strength this build requires. Do not use PLA or standard PETG — they can fail under string tension over time.">
        Two spools are required for a complete body. See [Print](/relay/body/print) for why material choice matters.
    </BomItem>
    <BomItem title="G/Flex 650 Epoxy" href="https://amzn.to/41jYduJ" source="Amazon" itemKey="gflex-650" fallback="$32.00" specific>
        A flexible marine-grade epoxy that handles vibration and stress without cracking. I've tested alternatives and come back to this every time. If you need to make a substitution, look for a slow-curing epoxy designed for plastic bonding.
    </BomItem>
</BomSection>

Everything else — hardware, electronics, neck — is in the instrument parts list for each model.
