Assembly is everything between a printed-and-finished body and a playable guitar: wiring the harness, mounting the hardware, attaching the neck, and setting the instrument up. It's the last stage of the build, and it's where a pile of parts starts sounding like your decisions.

## What you can do today

The wiring documentation is live for every released voicing. Each guide is a bench-side reference — selector map, connection points, and the published harness — written so you can build and verify the circuit before it ever goes into the body:

- [Lipstick wiring guide](/relay/voicings/lipstick/wiring)
- [Velvet wiring guide](/relay/voicings/velvet/wiring)
- [Arc wiring guide](/relay/voicings/arc/wiring)
- [Torch wiring guide](/relay/voicings/torch/wiring)

Your parts should already be sorted from the [components page](/relay/components) — if not, pick your voicing there before you start soldering anything.

## What's coming

The rest of the assembly documentation — hardware mounting, neck attachment, stringing, and setup — is in development. I only publish steps I've validated on my own builds, and I'd rather hand you nothing than hand you instructions I haven't proven. Here's the shape of what's planned:

- Hardware install: bridge, tuners, strap buttons, output jack, ground
- Neck attachment and alignment checks
- Pickup install and height adjustment
- Stringing, intonation, and action setup

Until those land, one standing rule serves you well: **if a step feels irreversible and something seems off, stop and ask before committing.** Post photos. It's much easier to prevent a mistake than to repair one.
