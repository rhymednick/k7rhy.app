## Voice and character

Relay Current is **punchy, cutting, and immediate**. It is less warm than Velvet, less spacious than Arc, and less throaty than Torch. The design target is **speed, edge definition, and strong projection** — notes that **jump out of the amp** with crisp upper-mid presence. The **3-way family** treats the middle **Hot Nashville** as a **curated auxiliary layer**: the fast, cutting alternate voice that defines the model when you add it in.

Current should feel **responsive and quick**, with **strong transient definition** and less bloom than Velvet or Arc — pointed, not ice-picky. The sharpest non-metal tool in the lineup: percussive rhythm work with clarity and momentum.

## Who builds this

Players who want **fast attack**, **percussive rhythm**, and **the line's sharpest non-metal tool** — funk, pop, and rock rhythm guitarists who hear "tight" and "forward" as compliments. If you need to sit on top of a busy groove with clarity and momentum, Current is aimed at you.

## Configuration

|                   |                                                         |
| ----------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Bridge**        | Humbucker (Alnico V HOT)                                |
| **Middle**        | Hot Nashville — sharpened auxiliary layer               |
| **Neck**          | Humbucker (Vintage 59)                                  |
| **Selector**      | 3-way toggle — bridge / bridge+neck / neck              |
| **Master volume** | 500k audio taper, full-size, **push-push**              |
| **Master tone**   | 500k audio taper, full-size, **push-pull**              |

## Controls

**3-way toggle**

1. Bridge  
2. Bridge + neck  
3. Neck  

Same control family as Lipstick: the middle is a layer you add, not a democratic third main pickup on the switch.

**Volume (push-push)** — Adds the Hot Nashville middle.

**Tone (push-pull)** — Engages the snap / bite contour.
