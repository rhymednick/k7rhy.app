This page covers the minimum setup needed to get the guitar playable. It is not a full professional setup — it's enough to verify the instrument works and to play it while you decide whether to take it further yourself or bring in a luthier.

## What you need

- Guitar tuner (clip-on or app)
- Capo (helpful for nut slot checks)
- String action gauge (a ruler works in a pinch)
- Screwdrivers for bridge saddle adjustment
- Allen wrenches (for truss rod and bridge saddles depending on parts)

## Setting relief

Neck relief is the slight forward bow in the neck under string tension. Too much bow = high action and poor feel. Back-bow = buzzing everywhere.

## When to stop here

If the guitar plays in tune, holds pitch, doesn't buzz excessively, and the electronics function — you're done for now. That's a playable instrument. Everything in [Optimization](/docs/relay/setup/optimization) is refinement, not requirement.
