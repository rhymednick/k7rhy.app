## Voice and character

Relay Hammer is the **least subtle** guitar in the family and does not pretend otherwise. **High-gain authority**, **tight aggressive attack**, and **rail-style passive pickups** — the **specialty brute** of the line, not the centerpiece. A **5-way Super Switch (4-pole)** matches how many metal players use the instrument: **direct access** to distinct combinations with headroom for position-specific routing, coil-mode logic, or aggressive high-gain wiring as the design matures.

Hammer should not be burdened with elegance. It should be **direct, forceful, and immediately legible** to the player it is built for: tight low end, saturated sustain under heavy picking, and a guitar that owns the high-gain lane without apology.

## Who builds this

**Metal and hard rock** players who want **tight low end**, **saturated sustain under heavy picking**, and a Relay that **owns** the high-gain lane. If you spend most of your time with gain staged and palm-muted and want a deliberate contrast to the warmer, more vintage-leaning siblings, Hammer is aimed at you.

## Configuration

|                   |                                                                 |
| ----------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Bridge**        | Rail humbucker (Crunchy Rails)                                  |
| **Middle**        | Rail humbucker (Power Rails) — core voice                       |
| **Neck**          | Rail humbucker (Crunchy Rails)                                  |
| **Selector**      | 5-way Super Switch, 4-pole                                      |
| **Master volume** | 500k audio taper, full-size, **push-push**                      |
| **Master tone**   | 500k audio taper, full-size, **push-push** or **push-pull**     |

## Controls

**5-way Super Switch** — Direct pickup access with room for future position-specific wiring logic (the Super Switch body is **deeper** than a standard blade; leave enough depth in the control cavity before you freeze the route).

**Volume (push-push)** — Best reserved for a fast, aggressive utility function.

**Tone (push-push or push-pull)** — Secondary high-gain mode or attack mode.
