The Relay Lipstick model is designed to be remixed and customized. This page describes the safe boundaries.

## What's fair game

- **Color:** Print in any color PET-CF or PET-GF you can source. The model looks particularly good in matte black and earth tones, but that's entirely your call.
- **Surface finish:** Post-processing with sandpaper and filler primer is fine. The outer surfaces are not structurally significant.
- **Control cavity cover:** This part has no structural role and can be freely redesigned.
- **Strap button locations:** The strap button positions can be moved in the model. The upper strap button location affects balance significantly — experiment before you bond.
- **Body contours:** Cosmetic contouring of the outer body profile is fine as long as you don't reduce wall thickness in the critical areas below.

## What affects structural integrity

These areas require careful thought before modifying:

<DecisionNote variant="caution" title="Don't reduce wall thickness around the neck pocket">
The neck pocket walls bear the majority of the string tension load. Reducing wall thickness here — even slightly — can cause cracking under tension. If you're redesigning this area, maintain at least [TODO: minimum wall dimension] mm of wall thickness on all sides.
</DecisionNote>

<DecisionNote variant="caution" title="Don't move the bridge mount without recalculating scale length">
The bridge mount position is set by the 24.75" scale length. Moving it changes intonation in ways that can't be corrected at the saddles. If you're designing a variant with a different scale length, every dimension from nut to bridge must be recalculated.
</DecisionNote>

## Remixing for the platform

If you're publishing a remix, I'd ask that you:

1. Credit the Relay platform and link back to this documentation
2. Test the build yourself before publishing
3. Note in your model description which parts of this documentation apply to your variant

The goal is that anyone who picks up a Relay derivative can find their way back here and get the support they need.
