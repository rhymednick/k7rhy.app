At the end of body construction you'll have a single component: a raw, bonded body ready to be sanded and sealed. Two materials get you there.

<BomSection>
    <BomItem title="PET-CF Filament (×2 spools)" href="https://amzn.to/4uIaVRl" source="Amazon" itemKey="pet-cf-filament" fallback="$66.00" specific
        substitution="PET-GF (glass fiber filled) is a valid alternative to PET-CF and may be easier to source. Both provide the dimensional stability and strength this build requires. Do not use PLA or standard PETG — they will fail under string tension over time."
    >
        Two spools required for a complete body. See [Printing Overview](/docs/relay/printing/overview) for why material choice matters.
    </BomItem>
    <BomItem title="G/Flex 650 Epoxy" href="https://amzn.to/41jYduJ" source="Amazon" itemKey="gflex-650" fallback="$32.00" specific>
        A flexible marine-grade epoxy that handles vibration and stress without cracking. I've tested alternatives and come back to this every time. Generic epoxy is not a substitute.
    </BomItem>
</BomSection>

Everything else — hardware, electronics, neck — is in the instrument parts list for each model.
