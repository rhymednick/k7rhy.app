All Relay models use the same fundamental circuit topology: three pickups, a 5-way selector, two push-push volume pots with coil split, a tone control with treble bleed, and a standard output jack. The specific pickups and selector positions vary by model, but the architecture is shared.

The central design challenge is making a lipstick pickup work harmoniously with humbuckers. The output levels, impedance characteristics, and frequency response of these pickup types are meaningfully different. This circuit is designed to make them coexist without one dominating the others.

## The push-push pots

The volume pots double as coil splits when pushed. This gives you access to single-coil sounds from the humbuckers without adding extra controls.

<DecisionNote title="Why push-push instead of a separate switch?">
A separate coil split switch would require drilling another hole in the body and adds a control that most players don't reach for often. Push-push keeps the panel clean and puts the split function on a control you're already using.
</DecisionNote>

## The treble bleed

The treble bleed network preserves high-frequency response when rolling back the volume. Without it, the guitar gets increasingly muddy as you reduce volume — an artifact of how passive volume pots interact with pickup inductance.

## Shielding

The control cavity is lined with copper foil tape and grounded. This is not optional — the lipstick pickup is sensitive to interference and an unshielded cavity will pick up significant noise. See [Wiring](/docs/relay/electronics/wiring) for the shielding process.
