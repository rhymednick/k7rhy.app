**Relay Lipstick** (the documented reference build) uses three pickups, a **3-way** selector for the **neck and bridge humbuckers**, two push-push volume pots with coil split, a tone control with treble bleed, and a standard output jack. The **lipstick** is wired so you can **blend it in** for tonal shaping on top of the humbucker voices — it is not treated as a stand-alone “third position” you select alone.

Other planned Relay models may use different selectors or voicing strategies; the shared idea is balancing a **character middle pickup** with humbuckers. The central design challenge is making a lipstick work harmoniously with humbuckers — output levels, impedance, and frequency response differ meaningfully. This circuit keeps the humbuckers authoritative while the lipstick adds color and detail when you want it.

## The selector

On **Relay Lipstick**, the **3-way** toggles the **humbuckers**: neck, both, bridge. Lipstick engagement and automatic bridge split for balance are handled in the **volume / switching** wiring, not as extra stops on the blade.

## The push-push pots

The volume pots double as coil splits when pushed. This gives you access to single-coil sounds from the humbuckers without adding extra controls.

<DecisionNote title="Why push-push instead of a separate switch?">
A separate coil split switch would require drilling another hole in the body and adds a control that most players don't reach for often. Push-push keeps the panel clean and puts the split function on a control you're already using.
</DecisionNote>

## The treble bleed

The treble bleed network preserves high-frequency response when rolling back the volume. Without it, the guitar gets increasingly muddy as you reduce volume — an artifact of how passive volume pots interact with pickup inductance.

## Shielding

The control cavity is lined with copper foil tape and grounded. This is not optional — the lipstick pickup is sensitive to interference and an unshielded cavity will pick up significant noise. See [Wiring](/docs/relay/electronics/wiring) for the shielding process.
