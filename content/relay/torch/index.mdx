## Voice and character

Relay Torch is the **rude, vocal-mid** model — closer to how many players hear **"alive" and "hooky"** in modern music. A **P90-type middle** (Mean 90 class) sits in the **5-way family** as a **core position**: not polite background texture, but a character you can lean on for rhythm hooks, aggressive cleans, and modern rock energy without defaulting to scooped high-gain clichés.

Torch should feel **current, punchy, and a little dangerous** — the guitar that **talks back** when you dig in. Midrange that sings and an instrument that feels emotionally direct. Attitude and edge with the broadest **immediate** appeal in the non-metal part of the lineup.

## Who builds this

Players who want **attitude**, **vocal mids**, and a sound that **cuts and hooks** — rock, pop, alternative, and modern country — anyone who wants **midrange that sings** and an instrument that feels emotionally direct. If you like the amp to feel like an extension of your picking hand and you want the line's broadest **immediate** appeal without going metal, Torch is aimed at you.

## Configuration

|                   |                                                                 |
| ----------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Bridge**        | Humbucker (VEH)                                                 |
| **Middle**        | P90-type (Mean 90) — core voice                                 |
| **Neck**          | Humbucker (Alnico II)                                           |
| **Selector**      | Standard 5-way blade, 2-pole (for now)                          |
| **Master volume** | 500k audio taper, full-size, standard (no push switch)        |
| **Master tone**   | 500k audio taper, full-size, **push-pull**                      |

## Controls

**5-way blade**

1. Bridge  
2. Bridge + middle  
3. Middle  
4. Neck + middle  
5. Neck  

The middle is too important to hide behind an “add middle” function. A standard 5-way stays enough unless Torch later gains position-specific tricks (forced split, per-position rerouting, and similar).

**Tone (push-pull)** — Engages the Torch edge contour or a bridge partial split, depending on the final voicing choice.
