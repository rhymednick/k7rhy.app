## What this is

The Relay Lipstick is a 3D-printed electric guitar body with a custom electronics design built around a lipstick pickup paired with two humbuckers. The body is inspired by the classic double-cutaway silhouette, scaled to a 24.75" neck. The model is free. The instructions are here.

This is not a "replace a Telecaster body with this print" project. This is a ground-up build with sourcing guidance, constraint explanations, and a community behind it. The goal is a functional instrument — one you understand well enough to finish, adjust, and if needed, hand off to a professional at the right moment.

## Who this is for

You don't need to be an experienced luthier. You do need to be comfortable with:

- 3D printing with structural materials (not PLA)
- Basic hand tools and patience during assembly
- Learning electronics fundamentals, or being willing to follow a wiring diagram carefully
- Accepting that some steps may require outside help

If you've printed models before but never finished a build into a working instrument, this documentation is designed specifically for you.

A successful build is an instrument capable of being set up and played — not necessarily one where you do every step yourself. My first Relay Lipstick was assembled and set up by a luthier. That's a completely valid outcome.

## Before you print

Read [Bill of Materials](/docs/relay/lipstick/planning/bom) and [What Will Fit](/docs/relay/lipstick/planning/compatibility) before you start printing anything. Sourcing the right neck and hardware is the step most likely to stall a build. Know what you need before the body is finished.

## If you get stuck

The <DiscordLink /> is the right place for build questions. Post photos, describe what you're seeing, and I'll engage. This is not a formal support service, but it's an active community and I'm in it.

## Remixers

If you've arrived here from a derivative or remixed model, all of this documentation applies to any build based on the Relay platform. The constraints around neck dimensions, pickup pairing, and material choice are platform-level — they don't change with the remix. Please credit the original model in your derivative.
