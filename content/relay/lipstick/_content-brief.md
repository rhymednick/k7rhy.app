## Background

I have conveived of a 3D printed guitar platform that I'm calling Relay. The goal is to develop a brand for K7RHY Resonance Lab that caters to the DIY audience. My real brand is help, support, and education through experimentation and development. With this platform, I hope to draw people from the 3D model sites to my website so that they can learn more about what I have to offer. Eventually, I will sell build kits and one-of-a-kind instruments that I build in partnership with a local artist (painter). The constructed instruments will be a new platform that doesn't exist yet.

Currently, if you go to one of these model sites and print one of the models, you're on your own in figuring out how to construct it. Typically, they say things like, "buy a Telecaster Benton kit and replace the body with this model." The problem is, this kit isn't really available in the US, so you're left to guess at what parts might work. I've had to abandon about half of my printed models because I wasn't able to figure out how to finish constructing them into a functional instrument. This is how I intend to differentiate myself; I will give you a complete end-to-end plan that will bring you from print to functional instrument. If you're not able to source the same parts, my content will help you understand what you're looking for so that you can find a local alternative, or even adapt the system to your own design goals and go in a different direction. Not only will I provide comprehensive documentation, I will give you a Discord community to ask questions and connect with other builders. I will monitor that community, but I am not committing to providing free support, I'm simply an experienced and supportive participant.

Builders will find this model organically, perhaps from search, or perhaps from external sites like MakerWorld or Printables. I will encourage people to remix the model because I know that my comprehensive instructions will bring all the new users back to me, and I will happily support anyone who choses to extend this design as long as they're preserving the platform identity by crediting me properly. This is my hook.

Printing and building a functional instrument is very challenging and not everyone will be able to do it. I will do my best to minimize the number of abandoned builds. I want people to be succeesful.

## The Guitar

**What is the Relay Lipstick?**

This is the first model in the platform. The Relay is a body style inspired by the PRS Santana model, but I can't say that because I don't want to risk trademark infringement. That is a HH guitar. I've added a lipstick pickup and customized the electronics to support it in a harmonious way (as this is not simple thing to do). That's why I call this the "Relay Lipstick."

**What format does it come in?**

Initially, I will tell people how to build the kit and aquire the parts to construct it. If there is sufficient interest, I will sell parts kits. The model is now and will always be free, as will be participation in my community, and all derivatives of the model.

**Where do people get it?**

People can get the model anywhere. I'll share it at every site I can find and host it on my own.

## The Builder

**Who is the target builder?**

I am assuming that the builder is a beginner, but I won't baby them through this difficult process. Rather, I will give them the information they need to be empowered to push through and learn what they need.

**What do they typically struggle with?**

There will be struggles at every turn and I will do my best to minimize them. First, they'll struggle to print it cleanly, but this will be greatly minimized by my guidance through material choices. Next, they'll struggle to assemble it, but I'll guide them through that too. When they're ready to add the electronics, that part won't be especially hard, but if they don't have prior experience then that'll be rough. Still, I'll help. Attaching the neck will be especially rough. I'll guide them, but also suggest that this might be a point where they can all in a luthier if they're overwhelmed. Then god only knows how successful they'll be with the final setup. I'll describe the steps, but I have no idea how likely people will be to complete that successfully.

**What does success look like for them?**

Success will be a produced instrument that's capapble of being made playable, even if they need to bring in professional support. For my first print, I took all of the parts and gave them to a lutier to do the final assembly.

## Your Philosophy

**What should this documentation NOT do?**

I don't want to explain everything. I want to give them just enough to be successfull.

**What decisions do you want builders to own?**

I want to tell them what they need to watch out for and ways they are free to improvise and ways they shouldn't. As in, what materials to avoid. What pickups to avoid. etc

**What constraints are non-negotiable?**

Nothing is non-negotiable, but they should know the consequences of their decisions. Some key details:

- I strongly suggest they print with PET-CF or PET-GF for stability and strength.
- I will provide recommendations for pickup resistance values and types and explain why deviating will be a problem. There are very few that will work when you put a lipstick in the mix. Specifically, they need to be PAF moderate output pickups. Hot pickups won't pair well with the lipsticks.
- The scale length is 24.75", so that's not negotiable. The neck has to have certain strict dimensions in order to fit in the body. I've bought several with the exact same specs and they vary, so some have required modification.
- There's much more flexibility in the choice of controls, but I've learned that it's not a good idea to move very far from what an experienced player expects a control to do.
