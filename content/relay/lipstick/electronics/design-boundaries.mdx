This is one of the most important pages in the Relay documentation. The circuit design isn't arbitrary — it's the result of iterating on the specific challenge of making a lipstick pickup work alongside humbuckers. This page describes the decision boundaries so you can understand where you have room to experiment and where you don't.

## Pickup selection

This is where most builds go wrong.

<DecisionNote variant="caution" title="Use PAF-style moderate-output humbuckers — not hot pickups">
High-output humbuckers overwhelm the lipstick signal even at matched volume settings. The lipstick has a characteristic sound that gets buried when the humbuckers are significantly louder. PAF-style pickups (DC resistance roughly 7–8kΩ) are what the circuit is balanced around. The GFS Classic II (neck) and Vintage 59 (bridge) are the specific models I've tested. If you substitute, stay in this output range.
</DecisionNote>

<DecisionNote variant="caution" title="The lipstick is not interchangeable with a standard single-coil">
Lipstick pickups have a specific impedance and output characteristic that's meaningfully different from a standard Strat-style single-coil. The tone and treble bleed values in this circuit are tuned for the GFS Pro Tube lipstick. Substituting a different lipstick may work but expect a different tonal balance.
</DecisionNote>

## What you can change

**Pickup output level (within range):** Any humbucker in the 7–9kΩ range should work. Above 10kΩ starts causing balance problems.

**Tone cap value:** The cap value changes where the tone control cuts. The BOM value gives a warm but not muddy roll-off. A smaller value cuts higher frequencies; a larger value cuts lower. This is a safe experiment — caps are cheap and soldering them is easy.

**Control layout:** You can change the function of the controls as long as the core topology (volume → tone → output) is preserved. Adding a mid-control is possible but requires passive circuitry knowledge.

## What you should not change

**The selector switch type:** The 5-way selector provides specific series and parallel combinations that make the lipstick usable in each position. Substituting a 3-way switch loses positions; substituting a 5-way of a different configuration may produce unexpected behavior.

**Eliminating the treble bleed:** Without it, the guitar loses presence when you roll back volume. It's a small component and worth installing.

**Skipping the cavity shielding:** The lipstick is sensitive. An unshielded cavity in this configuration will pick up significant hum and interference.

## If you deviate

That's fine — this is your instrument. Document what you changed and if you run into problems, share the changes when you ask for help in the <DiscordLink />. "The circuit doesn't work" is hard to debug; "I used [X] pickup and changed [Y]" is not.
