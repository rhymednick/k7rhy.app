Relay is a family of 3D-printed electric guitars built around a shared body platform and a consistent design philosophy. Every model in the line uses the same body architecture, the same neck format, and the same three-pickup layout. What changes between models is the pickup selection — and with it, the tonal character of the instrument.

This is not a single design with cosmetic variations. Each model is tuned to a distinct emotional center. The goal is a family of instruments where each one has a reason to exist.

## Why three pickups

Most electric guitars give you two voices: neck and bridge. The three-position selector puts you in one, the other, or a blend of both. That middle position is typically a compromise — softer, a little hollow, a transition point between two real destinations.

Relay guitars are built differently. The three-pickup layout reserves a **character pickup** in the middle of the body — chosen per model — so the instrument is not limited to two humbucker endpoints. How that pickup is used depends on the model: some lineups lean on multi-position switching; **Relay Lipstick** keeps neck and bridge humbuckers on a **3-way** foundation and uses the lipstick as a **blend for shaping**, not as a standalone voice you live in by itself.

The goal across the family is the same: three pickups worth of tonal material, arranged so the guitar has a clear identity without feeling like a compromise.

## Why 24.75" scale

The 24.75" scale length comes from the Gibson tradition — slightly shorter than the Fender standard at 25.5", with a little less tension across the strings. That reduced tension translates to a slightly easier feel under the fingers, a touch more give when bending, and a harmonic character that sits on the warmer side of neutral. For instruments built around expressiveness and dynamic response rather than snap and brightness, it's the right fit.

There's also a practical reason that matters more than it might seem: Les Paul-style necks are everywhere. The 24.75" scale is the standard for a huge portion of the bolt-on neck market, which means more options, better availability, and lower prices. Building the platform around this scale length keeps sourcing straightforward — you're not hunting for something obscure every time you want to start a build.
