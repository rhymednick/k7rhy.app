Relay is an electric guitar you print at home on a conventional 3D printer. Every model shares the same basic body shape. Each version changes the pickup layout and customizes the controls to match. The goal is a stable base you can trust for assembly and wiring. Each model is aimed at a different musical focus. This is not the same guitar with a fresh parts list. 

I publish pickup and control recommendations and explain the reasoning behind them. Treat those as defaults, not rules—change whatever suits you. If you want a second opinion on a tweak, ask me and other builders in our <DiscordLink>Discord community</DiscordLink>.

[Relay Lipstick](/docs/relay/lipstick) is ready to build today, with docs from files through setup. The rest are still in progress. Jump to [Models in the lineup](#models-in-the-lineup) below to see the roadmap and vote for what gets finished next.

## What stays the same on every model

### Three pickups

I like the sound of two humbuckers, but I don't want to swap pickups every time I want a different voice. A third pickup adds that extra dimension. You get room for more tonal blends than a typical two-humbucker layout. 

On every model the middle pickup earns its keep in a specific way. Sometimes it is a full third voice. Sometimes it serves to shape the other two. Still, the platform doesn't require you to use a third pickup. If you prefer not to install it, use the optional covers included with the models to hide the cavity.

### 24.75 inch scale

**Scale length** is the span from the bridge to the nut (the small bar at the top of the neck). It defines how long the vibrating part of each string is.

Relay uses 24.75 inches. That is a little shorter than the common Stratcaster length (25.5 inches). Shorter scale means lower string tension. Bends can feel a little easier, and the tone often leans warmer than on a longer-scale guitar. Lower tension also helps a printed body. Plastic and glue don't take brute force the way solid wood does.

The length is fixed for this platform. Pair these bodies with a neck built for the same scale. If you mix scales, the guitar will not play in tune.

Necks for this length are easy to find. Many suppliers stock them, which means less hunting, more variety, and often a lower price.
