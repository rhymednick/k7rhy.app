Relay is an electric guitar you print at home on a conventional 3D printer. Every model of Relay guitar shares the same basic body shape, a double-cut body with cavities for three pickups. Three pickup cavities give us the ability to build a wide array of models with distinct voices and personalities. I've designed the models such that they have a **clear emotional center** and a **simple, understandable control layout**. 

For each model, I publish pickup and control recommendations and explain the reasoning behind them. Treat those as defaults, not limits, and feel free to make decisions that suit your taste. If you want a second opinion on a tweak, ask me and the other builders in our <DiscordLink>Discord community</DiscordLink>.

[Relay Lipstick](/docs/relay/lipstick) is the model that I used to work out the kinks in the platform and it's ready to build today. I provide documentation to guide you through the entire build process. The other models have been designed and are in various stages of testing. You can't just put random pickups together and expect them to sound good. You need to find the right balance between them and then tune the electronics to create the sound you're going for. 

Jump to [Models in the lineup](#models-in-the-lineup) below to see the roadmap and vote for your favorites. I'll prioritize the ones that are receiving the most interest.

## About the Models

### Three Pickups

I like the sound of two humbuckers, but the number of voices you can get from a guitar with two matched humbuckers are limited. A third pickup adds another dimension of sound to work with, but it doesn't always work in the way you might expect. 

On every model the middle pickup earns its keep in a specific way. **Selector strategy follows that job:** models where the middle is a **curated auxiliary or shaping layer** use a **3-way** switch (bridge, bridge+neck, neck) with the middle engaged by its own control where needed. Models where the middle deserves to be a **core pickup position** use a **5-way** blade so you can reach the middle-based combinations directly. That keeps the platform musical instead of forcing one switch logic everywhere.

The platform doesn't require you to use a third pickup. If you prefer not to install it, use the optional covers included with the model files to hide the cavity.

### 24.75" Scale

**Scale length** is the span from the bridge to the nut (the small bar at the top of the neck). It defines how long the vibrating part of each string is.

Relay uses 24.75 inches. That is a little shorter than the common Stratcaster length of 25.5 inches. Shorter scale means lower string tension. Bends feel a little easier, and the tone often leans warmer than on a longer-scale guitar. Lower tension also helps a printed body; plastic and glue don't handle the forces the same way wood does. 

Another reason I picked this scale lenth is because necks of this scale are easy to find. This is the standard Les Paul length. Many suppliers stock them, which means less hunting, more variety, and often a lower price.
